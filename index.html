As 2022 Ends, 'Instrumental' Crime Surpasses Murder and Rape in NYC

By Caroline Handel

Dec. 5, 2022

If you’ve watched the local news or read a local paper, you’ve probably heard that all crime is on the rise in NYC.

But the data tells a different story.

From 2019 to 2022 (data year-to-date), the number of reported citywide murders so far this year remained relatively small, increasing from 319 to 393. Rape cases in that same time period actually decreased.

According to Christopher Herrmann, an associate professor at John Jay College of Criminal Justice, violent felonies tend to be low in volume. Herrmann designates felonies into two categories: ‘expressive’, or violent crimes and ‘instrumental’ crimes, which tend to have a financial component attached.

Felony assault was an outlier in 2022. Herrmann attributes this to a continuation of a pandemic trend of elevated alcohol use, which can increase aggression. An increased trend of subway assaults that began in 2021 also caused the number to spike.

“There are not many 'benefits' to violent crimes or expressive crimes,” Herrmann said. “Instrumental crimes, like carjackings, get you a car for a few days or weeks or years. Or maybe you sell the car for parts, or use it to commit another crime.”

G.L.A (Grand Larceny of a Vehicle) outstripped other types of instrumental crimes. NYPD saw a huge increase in incidents, from 5,430 in 2019 to 12,596 so far this year.
